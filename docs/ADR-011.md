# ADR-011: Governance High Conviction Requirement

## Context
To prevent malicious proposals from short-term holders, the Founder requires that only users with high conviction can submit a proposal. A proposal threshold of 10,000 $JNSX is already in place. However, an additional restriction is required: the proposer must have an active lock of at least 365 days.

## Decision
For the current local testing and iteration phase, the `proposalThreshold` is set to 10,000 $JNSX in `JNSGovernorzk.sol`.
The strict 1-year lock requirement (`hasActiveLockOfAtLeast(user, 365 days)`) is documented here and **will be added in the final mainnet deployment**. This avoids breaking ABI compatibility and forcing a full re-deployment of `JNSStaking` and `JNSGovernorzk` during this fast UI/UX local iteration phase.

## Consequences
- Local tests will rely on the 10,000 $JNSX threshold for proposal creation.
- The final smart contract audit must verify that `propose()` checks the `JNSStaking` contract for `lockType >= DAYS_365`.
